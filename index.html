About

Scholar.
Strategist.
Practitioner.

PhD in Media and Information Warfare Studies. Over a decade shaping how Malaysia thinks about influence, propaganda, and strategic communications — in classrooms, war rooms, and boardrooms alike.

Affiliated with the Centre for Media and Information Warfare Studies (CMIWS) at UiTM, where the work goes beyond theory — into building real intelligence systems for real decision-makers.

A growing body of work now sits at the intersection of Human-Computer Communication (HCC) — exploring how humans and AI systems communicate, collaborate, and co-produce intelligence. This theoretical lens underpins the RADAR framework, positioning human judgment not as a checkpoint, but as the critical communicator within an automated intelligence pipeline.

Research anchored in visual framing, political participation, cognitive influence, and the psychology of persuasion — applied directly to government communications strategy, counter-narrative operations, and AI-powered intelligence platforms.
